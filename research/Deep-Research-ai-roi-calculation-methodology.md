# AI ROI Calculation Methodology — Consultancy & Analyst Frameworks (2025-2026)

**Researched:** 2026-03-20
**Prompt:** Research how major consultancies (McKinsey, Deloitte, Accenture) and analysts (Gartner, Forrester) frame AI automation ROI for businesses. Cover calculation inputs, quantification methods for time saved vs decisions improved vs revenue impact, industry/function benchmarks, partial automation framing, credible ROI ranges, and publicly available ROI calculators. Purpose: building a credible, defensible AI assessment tool for SMBs.

## Key Findings

- **Forrester's SMB-specific TEI study** (up to 300 employees) found **132-353% ROI over 3 years**, NPV of $358K-$955K, 18% employee satisfaction increase, and 11-20% reduction in employee churn — the strongest anchor for SMB-targeted claims.
- **The majority experience is modest**: 68% of AI-using employees save 4 hours or less per week. Only 27% of frequent users save 9+ hours/week. Conservative framing (2-4 hrs/week) is the defensible baseline.
- **Process redesign is the multiplier**: McKinsey's end-to-end AI integration yields up to 25% cost savings vs ~5% for isolated experiments. Deloitte finds companies that redesign work are 2x more likely to exceed ROI expectations. PwC's 80/20 rule: technology delivers only ~20% of value; 80% comes from work redesign.
- **Function-level routine task percentages** are well-documented: Sales reps spend only 28-30% of time selling (70% on admin), Finance FP&A spends only 35% on insight generation, HR spends 57% on administrative tasks — these are the automatable baselines.
- **Credibility caveat**: 42% of companies abandoned most AI projects in 2025 citing cost/unclear value. Only 1% of companies report reaching AI maturity (McKinsey). Including these counterpoints builds trust in the assessment tool.

## Full Research

### 1. McKinsey's Frameworks for Calculating AI Value

**Core Methodology:**
McKinsey uses a function-by-function analysis of automation potential, measuring the percentage of work activities that can be automated and the economic value this represents. Their "3:1 Rule" emphasizes spending 3x more on process redesign than on the technology itself.

**FTE-Based Calculation Approach:**
McKinsey identifies individual use cases at the domain level, estimates the amount of time FTEs dedicate to each use case, assigns a ranged impact based on expert input, then applies a "capture rate" to account for value leakage and execution challenges.

**Productivity Gains by Function:**

| Function | Automation Potential / Value Impact |
|---|---|
| Customer Operations | 30-45% productivity increase (as % of current function costs) |
| Marketing & Sales | 3-5% increase of current global sales expenditure; 28% of total GenAI value |
| Software Engineering | 20-45% productivity improvement (as % of annual spending) |
| R&D | 10-15% of overall R&D costs |
| Finance | 20-30% reduction in manual data "drudgery" |
| HR | 15-20% cost reduction |
| Procurement | 25-40% more efficient (agentic AI) |

**Headline Numbers:**
- 60-70% of employee work activities could potentially be automated with current technology
- 57% of U.S. work hours are automatable with existing technology (November 2025 update)
- 75% of GenAI value concentrates in 4 functions: customer operations, marketing/sales, software engineering, R&D
- $4.4 trillion in annual productivity projected from corporate AI use cases
- Companies with end-to-end AI integration achieve cost savings up to 25%; isolated experiments achieve 5% or less
- High performers (6% of respondents) attribute 5%+ EBIT impact to AI
- While 92% of companies plan to increase AI investments in the next 3 years, only 1% report reaching AI maturity

### 2. Deloitte's AI ROI Models

**AI ROI Performance Index:**
Deloitte created a composite score combining four metrics: direct financial return, revenue growth from AI, operational cost savings, and speed of results. The top 20% are classified as "AI ROI Leaders."

**Key Findings (State of AI in Enterprise 2026):**
- 66% of organizations report productivity and efficiency gains from enterprise AI
- Only 20% are achieving revenue growth from AI (vs. 74% aspiring to)
- Satisfactory ROI typically achieved within 2-4 years; only 6% see payback in under a year
- 85% of organizations increased AI investment in the past 12 months; 91% plan to increase again
- Companies that redesign work are 2x more likely to exceed ROI expectations

**AI ROI Leader Characteristics:**
- 62% have AI explicitly part of corporate strategy
- 50% define critical AI wins as "creation of revenue growth opportunities"
- 43% cite "business model reimagination"
- 86% use different frameworks/timeframes for generative vs. agentic AI
- Only 16% have fully designed roles, processes, and operating models to integrate AI

**Work Redesign Emphasis:**
Deloitte stresses that productivity gains must be measured in operational terms (cycle time, throughput, quality) — not self-reported sentiment or subjective time savings.

### 3. Accenture's Approach

**Core Finding:**
Companies with AI-led processes achieve:
- 2.5x higher revenue growth
- 2.4x greater productivity
- 3.3x greater success at scaling GenAI use cases

**Maturity Framework:**
Accenture assessed 2,000 executives across 12 countries and 15 industries, categorizing businesses into four levels: Reinvention-ready, Insight-driven, Automated, and Foundational.

**Function-Level GenAI Deployment (among reinvention-ready companies):**
- IT: 75%
- Marketing: 64%
- Customer Service: 59%
- Finance: 58%
- R&D: 34%

**Scale of Transformation:**
The number of companies with fully modernized, AI-led processes nearly doubled from 9% (2023) to 16% (2024). 63% of organizations plan to strengthen GenAI and automation capabilities by 2026.

### 5. Key Inputs Used in ROI Calculations

The standard inputs consultancies use across frameworks:

**Labor Inputs:**
- Fully loaded employee cost (salary × 1.25-1.8 multiplier depending on role/region)
- US private industry: wages average $32.37/hr + benefits $13.68/hr = ~$46/hr total
- Regional variation (US): $39.94/hr (South) to $56.67/hr (Northeast)
- Benefits = 29.8% of total compensation in private industry
- Social Security: 6.2% (up to $168,600); Medicare: 1.45% (no cap)
- Health insurance: ~$8,400/year (single), ~$24,000/year (family)

**Time Allocation Inputs (by function):**
- **Sales:** Only 28-30% of time spent actually selling; 70% on admin, data entry, internal meetings. 43% of reps report admin occupies 10-20 hours/week
- **HR:** 57% of time spent on administrative tasks (Deloitte)
- **Finance:** Only 35% of FP&A time on high-value insight generation. 40% of businesses manage up to half their financial data manually. 72% of finance teams spend up to 520 hours/year on manual AP tasks
- **Knowledge workers (general):** 28% of week on email management, 20% searching for information/tracking down colleagues

**Error/Quality Inputs:**
- Error rates in manual processes (baseline)
- Compliance penalty risk
- Rework costs

**Decision Inputs:**
- Time to decision (cycle time)
- Decision quality (accuracy rate)
- Speed of response to customers/opportunities

### 6. Quantifying Different Value Types

#### "Time Saved" (Task Automation)

**Formula:** (Hours Saved per Task × Frequency per Year) × Fully Loaded Hourly Labor Cost

**Benchmarks:**
- Typical AI automation saves 25-50% of time on repetitive tasks
- Federal Reserve research: GenAI users save 5.4% of work hours weekly on average
- 27% of frequent AI users save 9+ hours/week
- More conservative reality: 68% of AI-using employees save 4 hours or less per week
- Email time reduced ~25% (~3 hours/week) per field experiment across 6,000 workers
- Australian consulting firms: employees save up to 7.5 hours/week on busywork
- CRM automation reduces admin time by 17%; saves 6 hours/week per sales rep

#### "Decisions Improved" (Better Outcomes)

- Financial planning: 12-15% better performance vs. manual-only analysis
- 52% of organizations report improved decision speed; 58% improved decision quality
- Only 3% report "major" speed gains — suggesting incremental rather than transformational improvements

#### "Revenue Impact" (Faster Response, Fewer Missed Opportunities)

- AI-enabled companies resolve customer tickets in 32 minutes vs. up to 36 hours
- Predictive churn models: 25% reduction in customer churn, 15% increase in retention
- CRM AI: 25% increase in sales, 30% increase in customer satisfaction
- Conversational AI projected to save $80 billion in contact-center labor costs by 2026
- AI chatbots resolve up to 70% of incoming queries without human intervention

### 8. Partial Automation Framing

The consultancies consistently frame AI as augmentation, not replacement:

**McKinsey's framing:** "Generative AI and other technologies have the potential to automate work activities that absorb 60-70% of employees' time" — but this means automating *portions* of roles, not eliminating them. Knowledge workers spend 20%+ of their time just searching for information; AI reclaims that time for higher-value work.

**Bain's "Zero-Based Redesign":** Leading companies achieve up to 25% cost savings by combining end-to-end process redesign with GenAI. The approach maps the current "point of departure" and reimagines the "point of arrival" with AI embedded. Without process redesign, basic AI tools deliver only ~10% gains.

**PwC's 80/20 rule:** Technology delivers only about 20% of an initiative's value; the other 80% comes from redesigning work so AI handles routine tasks and people focus on impact.

**Consulting economics example:** Time saved by AI gets reinvested into better client work. Consultants use that time to deepen client dialogue — projects deliver faster insights because teams spend less time compiling data and more time making sense of it.

**Harvard/BCG controlled experiment (758 knowledge workers):**
- 12.2% more tasks completed with AI access
- 25.1% faster task completion
- 40%+ higher quality outputs
- Bottom-half performers saw 43% boost (vs. 17% for top performers)
- For tasks outside AI capabilities, AI users were 19 percentage points *less* likely to produce correct solutions

### 9. Credible ROI Ranges

#### Cost Reduction
- **Conservative:** 5-10% (isolated AI experiments, no process redesign)
- **Moderate:** 15-25% (systematic deployment with some process redesign)
- **Aggressive:** 25-40% (end-to-end AI integration with zero-based redesign)
- **Function-specific peaks:** Customer service 30%, Financial compliance 40%, Marketing 37%

#### Hours Saved Per Employee Per Week
- **Conservative (majority experience):** 2-4 hours/week (68% of users report this level)
- **Moderate (active users):** 5-8 hours/week
- **High (power users with configured workflows):** 9-15 hours/week
- **Peak claims:** 20+ hours/week (only 27% of frequent users)

#### Decision Speed Improvement
- **General improvement:** 52% of organizations report improved decision speed
- **Major speed gains:** Only 3% report transformational improvement
- **Financial planning:** 12-15% better performance
- **Customer response:** From 36 hours to 32 minutes (best-in-class vs. laggards)

#### Overall ROI
- **Enterprise average:** $3.70 return per $1 invested
- **SMB (Forrester/Microsoft):** 132-353% over 3 years
- **Enterprise (Forrester/Microsoft):** 116% over 3 years
- **Agentic AI projections:** 171% average; 192% for US enterprises
- **Payback period:** 3-6 months (SMB automation), 2-4 years (enterprise-wide transformation)

#### Employee Satisfaction
- 18% increase in satisfaction (Forrester SMB study)
- 11-20% reduction in employee churn
- 75% of AI users say it increases productivity and work quality

### Recommended Calculation Methodology for SMB Assessment Tool

Based on this research, a credible and defensible calculation for an SMB (10-50 people) assessment tool:

**Inputs to collect:**
1. Number of employees by function (Finance, HR, Sales, Operations, Customer Service)
2. Average salary by function (or use defaults based on region)
3. Industry vertical

**Calculation layers:**
1. **Fully loaded cost** = Salary × 1.35 (conservative multiplier)
2. **Routine task percentage** by function (Sales: 70%, HR: 57%, Finance: 65%, Customer Service: 45%, Operations: 40%)
3. **AI automation capture rate** = 25-35% of routine task time (conservative, based on the 2-4 hours/week majority experience)
4. **Value of time saved** = Fully loaded hourly cost × hours recaptured
5. **Quality/error improvement multiplier** = 1.10-1.15x (10-15% better outcomes)
6. **Revenue impact multiplier** = 1.02-1.05x for customer-facing functions (based on Forrester's 2.7% pipeline improvement data)

**Conservative benchmarks to use (defensible):**
- 2-4 hours saved per employee per week (the 68% majority experience)
- 15-25% operational cost reduction (McKinsey's moderate range)
- 3-6 month payback period for SMB automation
- 132-353% ROI over 3 years (Forrester SMB study)

**Credibility notes:**
- Cite the Harvard/BCG study for the "40% quality improvement" claim — it is the most rigorous controlled experiment
- Use the Forrester TEI SMB study as the anchor for SMB-specific claims
- Frame results as ranges, not point estimates
- Include the "implementation quality" caveat: 42% of companies abandoned most AI projects in 2025 citing cost/unclear value

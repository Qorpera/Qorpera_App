# Digital Marketing Channels for Reaching Danish SMB Decision-Makers

**Researched:** 2026-03-20
**Prompt:** Research the most effective digital marketing channels and tactics for reaching Danish SMB decision-makers (CEOs, COOs of 10-200 employee companies), including platform benchmarks, SEO strategy, and Danish business media options — with a tactical budget allocation for 25,000 DKK driving traffic to a free AI assessment tool.

## Key Findings

- **Meta/Facebook is the best first-spend channel for Denmark.** Denmark CPC averages USD 0.76 (33% below global), CPM ~DKK 60. At B2B targeting rates (DKK 30–120 CPM), 15K DKK buys ~2,000–2,500 clicks. Strong seasonal pattern: Q1 expensive, Q3-Q4 dramatically cheap (CPC drops to $0.18–$0.31).
- **LinkedIn has exceptional Danish penetration (78% of 18+, 3.8M users) but brutal unit economics.** CPC $6–$15 for C-suite targeting (DKK 40–105+), CPM $33–65 (DKK 230–450). Entire 25K budget yields only ~350 clicks. Best used as organic channel at this budget level.
- **Danish-language SEO is a free lunch.** Virtually zero B2B SaaS competition in Danish. Google holds 90-91% market share in Denmark. 86% of Danes speak English but prefer Danish for local services. Publishing Danish content first yields near-zero competition vs. competing against the entire anglophone SaaS ecosystem in English.
- **Computerworld Denmark is the most accessible paid media for IT decision-makers.** Newsletter banners cost DKK 3,000–4,500 reaching 16-19K IT buyers. Advertorials DKK 20-30K. Børsen and Finans are premium/out-of-budget.
- **Recommended 25K DKK split:** 15K Meta ads (volume + data), 7-8K Computerworld newsletters (2 placements), 2K retargeting reserve. LinkedIn organic and Danish SEO as parallel zero-cost tracks. Skip Google Ads — $5.70/click with declining CTR doesn't work at this budget.

#### Budget Math

- Minimum viable budget: DKK 3,000/month for optimization data (Gezar.dk)
- Recommended serious investment: DKK 8,000–25,000/month (Gezar.dk)
- At DKK 60 CPM broad targeting: 25K DKK = ~416,000 impressions
- At DKK 80-100 CPM with B2B targeting + 0.8% CTR: ~2,000–2,500 clicks for 15K DKK

#### B2B Targeting Options

Job title targeting, company size filters, interest-based audiences (business software, ERP, CRM). Lookalike audiences from website visitors. In-platform lead forms reduce friction. Finance, insurance, real estate, and legal are the most expensive verticals.

#### Global Context

- Tier 1 markets (US, Australia, Canada, Western Europe): CPMs $10–$23
- Global median CPC: $1.11 (Jan 2025–Jan 2026), peak $1.32 in November, reset to $0.85 by January 2026
- B2B professional services CPC: $0.50 in low-competition markets to $3+ in high-value sectors

Source: WordStream, WebFX, AdAmigo.ai

### 3. Google Ads — B2B SaaS

#### B2B SaaS Benchmarks (2025)

| Metric | Search | Display |
|---|---|---|
| CPC | $5.70 | $2.80 |
| CTR | 3.2% | 0.9% |
| Conversion rate | 4.7% | 1.2% |
| CPA | $95 | $70 |

Source: AdLabz

#### Trend Data (Dreamdata, Copenhagen-based)

- Non-branded search CPC: jumped ~29% to $5.34 average (from $4.13)
- CTR: dropped ~26% to 4.04% average (from 5.47%)
- Non-branded search budget share: declined from 38.10% to 32.83% — marketers quietly shifting away
- CPC rose for 87% of industries over the past year

Source: Dreamdata B2B Google Search Benchmark

#### Cost Drivers

1. Quality Score degradation from lower CTRs triggering higher bid penalties
2. AI Overview placement pushing ads lower on search results pages
3. Campaign overlap between multiple Google ad types competing for same clicks

#### Denmark Considerations

Lower competition on Danish-language keywords ("AI virksomhedsstyring", "operationel intelligence") vs. English equivalents. But search volume is minimal — Denmark has 5.9M people, so long-tail B2B SaaS Danish keywords likely get 10-50 searches/month.

#### Budget Math at 25K DKK

- At DKK 40/click: ~625 clicks
- At 4.7% conversion rate: ~29 assessment starts
- CPA: ~DKK 862 per assessment start

### 4. SEO — Danish vs. English Strategy

#### Denmark Search Landscape

| Factor | Value |
|---|---|
| Google market share | 90–91% across devices |
| Mobile search share | ~95% |
| Mobile search usage | 85%+ of searches on mobile |
| English proficiency | 86% of Danes speak English |
| Preferred content language | Danish for local products/services |

Source: Besa Digital, RankTracker

#### Danish vs. English Comparison

| Factor | Danish | English |
|---|---|---|
| B2B SaaS competition | Very low to zero | High globally, moderate in DK |
| Search volume | Low (5.9M population) | Higher but diffuse |
| Language preference | Preferred for local services | Used in tech/business contexts |
| Compound words | Single-word compounds (e.g., "hjemmelevering") | Standard tokenization |
| Content cost | Higher (fewer writers) | Lower (global talent pool) |

#### Technical Considerations

- Danish compound words: users search "virksomhedsstyring" as one word, not spaced variants
- Mixed approach likely necessary: Danish for local/SMB targeting, English for tech-savvy audience
- 2025-2026 trend: AI-ready SEO (AEO/AI-SEO) becoming critical for visibility in AI answer boxes

Source: Besa Digital, RankTracker

### 7. Recommended 25,000 DKK Budget Allocation

| Channel | Budget (DKK) | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|
| Meta/Facebook Ads | 15,000 | ~2,000–2,500 targeted clicks |
| Computerworld newsletters (2x) | 7,000–8,000 | ~32K–35K IT decision-maker impressions |
| LinkedIn organic | 0 | Personal posts 3x/week, comment strategy |
| SEO/content (Danish) | 0 | 2 Danish blog posts/month (parallel track) |
| Retargeting reserve | 2,000 | Meta retargeting of assessment drop-offs |

#### Projected Pipeline

- 2,500 Meta clicks × 5-10% assessment start rate = 125–250 assessment starts
- 2 Computerworld placements × 35K impressions × 0.1% CTR = ~35 additional clicks
- Total first-month pipeline from paid: ~150–280 assessment starts

#### Rationale

1. **Meta gives data.** Cheapest clicks in Denmark, enough volume to A/B test audiences and messages.
2. **Computerworld newsletters give credibility.** 16-19K IT buyer subscribers at 3-4.5K DKK per placement.
3. **LinkedIn organic is free.** 78% adult penetration, CEO/COO content about AI operations for Danish SMBs.
4. **Danish SEO is uncontested.** Near-zero B2B SaaS competition in Danish. Compound returns in 3-6 months.
5. **Skip Google Ads.** $5.70/click with 29% YoY increase and declining CTR from AI Overviews.
6. **Skip LinkedIn paid.** DKK 70+/click for C-suite targeting exhausts budget in ~350 clicks.

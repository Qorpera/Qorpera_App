# Danish & Scandinavian SMB AI Adoption — Market Intelligence

**Researched:** 2026-03-20
**Prompt:** Current state of AI adoption among SMBs (10-200 employees) in Denmark and Scandinavia — adoption rates, tool usage, barriers, budgets, and industry segmentation, with citable data from Danish/Nordic institutional sources.

## Key Findings

- **Denmark is #1 in the EU for enterprise AI adoption** at 42% (2025), more than double the 20% EU average. AI usage tripled from 15% in 2023. Among 10-49 employee companies specifically: 37%. (Danmarks Statistik)
- **Adoption is broad but shallow.** 70% of AI-active SMVs primarily use it for text generation and routine tasks; only 6% have advanced applications. 95% of that usage is ChatGPT — 2-3x more popular than Copilot or Gemini. (DI, Dansk Erhverv)
- **The #1 barrier is competence, not cost.** 74% of SMV leaders cite employee skill gaps, 51% cite management understanding gaps, 6 in 10 say managers lack insight into AI's potential. (Dansk Erhverv, DI)
- **The SMV investment gap is widening.** Small companies (20-49 employees) expect a 2% decline in IT investment for 2026 — first time in 5 years — while 100+ employee companies increase 6.5-7%. (DI)
- **McKinsey estimates DKK 100-160 billion in extractable AI value** for Danish companies, with 1.6 pp annual GDP growth potential. 82% of current AI users report time savings, 7 in 10 report increased efficiency. (McKinsey, PA Consulting, Digitaliseringsstyrelsen)

## Full Research

### Overall AI Adoption Rates

#### Denmark

**Danmarks Statistik** (official government data, representative survey of companies with 10+ employees):
- AI usage tripled: 15% (2023) → 28% (2024) → 42% (2025)
- Highest AI adoption rate among all EU countries in both 2024 and 2025
- EU average approximately 20% in 2025 — Denmark more than double

**By company size (Danmarks Statistik 2025):**

| Company Size | 2023 | 2025 | Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10-49 employees | 10% | 37% | +27 pp |
| 50-99 employees | 16% | 56% | +40 pp |
| 100-249 employees | 22% | 62% | +40 pp |
| 250+ employees | 45% | 75% | +30 pp |

**Dansk Industri (DI) — March 2025 analysis:**
- One third of companies used AI at time of survey; half expected to by end of 2025
- DI called 2025 the year of the "ketchup effect" in AI adoption
- By June 2025 (DI Future of Work member survey): 72% of companies use generative AI (up from 43% in 2024)

**Dansk Erhverv — February 2026 analysis:**
- 70% of Dansk Erhverv members now use AI tools (up from 44% in 2023)
- Nearly all companies with 250+ employees (96%) use AI
- Only ~57% of smallest companies (0-9 employees) use AI

#### Sweden

- 30.8% of small enterprises (10-49 employees) use AI in 2025
- 49.6% of medium enterprises (50-249 employees)
- 71.9% of large enterprises (250+)
- ICT sector has reached 87.9% AI adoption (near saturation)

#### Norway

- 46.4% population-level AI usage (highest in Europe for individuals)
- Norwegian financial services sector leads with 85% AI usage (vs. 40% European financial industry average)

#### Eurostat EU-wide comparison (2025)

| Country | Enterprise AI adoption |
|---|---|
| Denmark | 42.0% |
| Finland | 37.8% |
| Sweden | 35.0% |
| EU average | 20.0% |

Denmark recorded the largest year-over-year increase: +14.5 pp from 2024 to 2025.

### Tools Being Used

**PA Consulting Denmark survey** (400 employees, large/medium Danish orgs):
- 56% of Danish companies provide access to generative AI (ChatGPT, Copilot, or bespoke tools) for employees
- 57% of employees surveyed use generative AI daily

**Dansk Erhverv:**
- 6 out of 10 businesses use ChatGPT and other language models
- ChatGPT is the dominant tool — 2-3x more popular than Google Gemini and Microsoft Copilot

**Danmarks Statistik — AI technology types used (2025):**
- Text analysis: grew from 6% to 29% of all companies (2023-2025)
- Text/speech production (gen AI): grew from 5% to 31%
- Workflow automation (software robots): most frequently used across all sectors
- Machine learning for data analysis: ~10% of companies
- Image/video/audio generation: growing but smaller share

**Microsoft Copilot global:**
- Paid M365 Copilot seats: 15 million (Q2 FY2026)
- Priced at $30/user/month (requires existing M365 license)

**Population level:**
- 48.4% of the Danish population uses generative AI tools (highest in EU)

### Barriers to Adoption

#### Competence & Skills Gap (barrier #1)

**Dansk Industri (DI) — August 2025:**
- 6 out of 10 companies say managers lack insight into generative AI's potential (biggest barrier)
- Nearly half say managers lack competencies to apply the technology and time to drive it forward
- Employees lack time and competencies to experiment with AI in practice
- 70% of AI-active SMVs primarily use it for text generation/routine tasks; only 6% have advanced applications

**Dansk Erhverv SMV analysis (Feb 2026):**
- 74% of SMV leaders cite lack of AI competencies among employees
- 51% highlight lack of AI understanding in management

**BCG Denmark survey** (572 executives, largest Nordic GenAI study):
- 71% of companies see insufficient talent as a bottleneck
- 55% of those are not even planning any workforce enablers
- Only 5% of companies are moving beyond pilot stage
- Only 21% planning to use the full breadth of GenAI

#### Trust, Data Security & Regulation

**PA Consulting Denmark:**
- 37% concerned about AI hallucinations/inaccuracies
- 22% cite ethical dilemmas
- 20% cite unpredictability
- 15% cite lack of transparency

**Dansk Erhverv SMV data:**
- 43% of SMVs view lack of ethical guidelines as a barrier
- 34% find legislation difficult to follow

**OECD (Nov 2025, across member countries):**
- 57% of non-adopters say AI is "unsuitable to their work"
- 54% concerned about copyright/legal/regulatory issues
- 52% worried about what happens to data fed into AI models
- 50% cite lack of employee skills

**EY Responsible AI Pulse Survey 2025** (975 C-suite, 120+ Nordic):
- 75% of Nordic CxOs say AI is integrated into all/most initiatives
- Only 26% of Nordic CEOs are involved in emerging tech strategy (vs. 49% globally)
- Gap between perceived readiness and actual governance maturity

#### EU AI Act

- Full application: 2 August 2026
- SMEs mentioned 38 times in the Act with dedicated support measures
- Regulatory sandboxes with SME priority access (free of charge)

#### Regional Inequality (Denmark-specific)

- Companies outside major cities struggle significantly more with AI adoption
- Spar Nord Fonden-funded 3-year research: major regional differences in ability to adopt AI

### Budgets & Investment

#### Danish IT Investment Data (DI, February 2026)

- Danish companies expect to increase IT investments by 5.1% in 2026 (DKK 5.5 billion increase)
- Companies with 100+ employees: expect 6.5-7% increase
- Small companies with 20-49 employees: expect a 2% decline in IT investment
- First time in 5 years SMVs enter a year with negative IT investment expectations

#### General SMB AI Spending

- SMBs globally expected to invest $460.5 billion in IT in 2026
- Small businesses (1-49 employees) spend 6.9% of revenue on IT (vs. 3.7% for enterprises)
- 78% of growing SMBs plan to increase AI spending (vs. 55% of declining SMBs)

#### Typical SME AI Implementation Costs

- Off-the-shelf AI tools: $20-$100/month per user
- Microsoft Copilot: $30/user/month (on top of M365 license)
- Small pilot projects: EUR 5,000-15,000 first year
- Strategic department-level solutions: EUR 20,000-75,000 first year
- AI spending grew 446% in the last 12 months among SMBs (vs. 36% growth in overall SaaS)

#### SaaS/AI Pricing Trends

- Companies like Notion, Slack, Loom charge $4-$10/user for AI add-ons
- Many now bundling AI and raising base prices by $2.50-$5/user
- SMBs (100-200 employees) spend approximately $251,119 annually on software total

### AI Adoption by Industry Segment

#### Denmark by Sector (Danmarks Statistik 2025)

| Sector | AI Adoption Rate |
|---|---|
| Information & Communication | 79% |
| Business Services (erhvervsservice) | 51% |
| Manufacturing (industri) | 39% |
| Trade & Transport | 38% |
| Construction | 24% |

#### Financial Services

- Danish financial sector: 81% daily generative AI usage (PA Consulting)
- Norwegian financial services: 85% AI usage (vs. 40% European average)
- 89% of Nordic financial sector respondents expect to increase AI use

#### B2B SaaS

- 70% of SaaS companies have some level of AI in their product
- 95% of organizations will adopt AI-powered SaaS applications
- AI investment climbed from 57% (2023) to 71% (2024) to 86% (2025) among professional services/agencies

#### Recruiting

- 43% of organizations worldwide used AI for HR/recruiting in 2025 (up from 26% in 2024)

### Productivity & ROI Impact

**Digitaliseringsstyrelsen (Danish government report, April 2025):**
- 7 out of 10 AI-using companies report increased efficiency
- Over half see improved products/services
- 1 in 3 report higher revenue, better decision-making, and improved customer contact

**PA Consulting Denmark:**
- 82% of employees say AI saves them time
- 40% report time savings of 5-15%
- 17% experienced savings exceeding 15%
- 93% expect AI adoption to increase at their workplace in the next 3 years

**Salesforce SMB Trends 2025** (3,350 respondents globally):
- 91% of SMBs with AI say it boosts revenue
- 87% say it helps scale operations
- 86% see improved margins
- 78% say AI will be a "game-changer" for their company

**DI productivity data:**
- Companies with a very high digital level have average value added per FTE that is 19% higher

---

### Economic Opportunity (Denmark)

**McKinsey — "An AI Nation?" report:**
- AI could add 1.6 percentage points in annual GDP growth for Denmark
- Approximately DKK 35 billion annually in productivity gains
- Danish companies could extract DKK 100-160 billion in value potential
- AI's potential is 2-3x bigger than first-wave web technologies and early robotics

**Implement Economics / IT-Branchen report (2024):**
- Generative AI potential in knowledge-intensive services: ~DKK 75 billion
- Manufacturing, construction, energy, water: ~DKK 50 billion

**Danish Government AI investment:**
- EUR 200 million committed to AI R&D
- Highest public investment in tech-driven R&D among OECD states (relative to GDP)
